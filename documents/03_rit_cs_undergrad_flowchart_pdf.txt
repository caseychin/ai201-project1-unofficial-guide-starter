RIT Computer Science BS — Undergraduate Course Flowchart
Source: RIT CS Department, CSUndergradFlowChart.pdf (manually transcribed)

This document describes the recommended course sequence for the RIT Computer Science BS degree, organized by year. Prerequisites are shown where relevant. Writing-intensive courses, co-op requirements, and general education requirements are noted throughout.

---

FIRST YEAR

First Semester:
- CSCI 141: Computer Science I (introductory programming; no prerequisite)
- MATH 181: Calculus I
- Gen Ed: 1st Year Writing Course (URWT 150 or ISTE 110 satisfies this requirement)
- Gen Ed: Artistic Perspective
- Gen Ed: Global Perspective
- YOPS 10: First-year orientation seminar

Second Semester:
- CSCI 142: Computer Science II (prerequisite: CSCI 141 with C or higher)
- MATH 182: Calculus II (prerequisite: MATH 181 with C or higher)
- MATH 190: Discrete Mathematics for Computing (co-requisite: MATH 182)
- Gen Ed: Ethical Perspective
- Gen Ed: Social Perspective
- Wellness Activity
- RIT 365: RIT and Society (1st Year Requirement)

---

SECOND YEAR

Third Semester:
- CSCI 243: The Art and Practice of Computer Science (prerequisite: CSCI 142 with C or higher; covers C programming, memory management, and software design)
- CSCI 262: Introduction to Computer Science Theory (prerequisite: CSCI 141 and MATH 190; covers formal languages, automata, and computability)
- MATH 251: Probability and Statistics (prerequisite: MATH 182)
- Lab Science 1 (Biology, Chemistry, or Physics sequence; see CS handbook for allowed sequences)
- Gen Ed Elective
- Co-op Seminar (CSCI 99; required every semester, prepares students for co-op search)

Fourth Semester:
- CSCI 261: Analysis of Algorithms (prerequisite: CSCI 243 and CSCI 262; covers algorithm design, complexity analysis, sorting, graphs, dynamic programming)
- MATH 241: Linear Algebra for Engineers (prerequisite: MATH 190; also listed as prerequisite for several AI/graphics electives)
- Lab Science 2 (continuation of the Lab Science sequence)
- Gen Ed: Natural Science Inquiry Perspective (Lab Science 1 fulfills this)
- Gen Ed: Mathematical Perspectives (fulfilled by Calculus sequence)
- Wellness Activity
- Summer Co-op (CSCI 488; optional but strongly encouraged; semester-length summer work experience)

---

THIRD YEAR

Fifth Semester:
- CSCI 331: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence (prerequisite: CSCI 142 and MATH 190)
- CSCI 320: Concepts of Parallel and Distributed Systems (prerequisite: CSCI 243 and MATH 190)
- CSCI 250: Computer Organization (prerequisite: CSCI 243 and MATH 190; covers digital logic, assembly language, memory hierarchy)
- Open Elective
- Gen Ed: Immersion (Immersion is a 3-course thematic sequence chosen from approved Gen Ed areas)
- Semester Co-op (CSCI 499; full-semester on-site or remote work experience)

Sixth Semester:
- Concurrent and Distributed Systems elective or CS Cluster Elective
- Language and Program Design (CSCI 344 or equivalent; prerequisite: CSCI 243 and MATH 243)
- Open Elective
- Gen Ed: Immersion continuation
- Semester Co-op (CSCI 499; another rotation if on co-op cycle)

---

FOURTH AND FIFTH YEAR

Seventh and Eighth (or Ninth) Semester:
- CS Cluster Elective (choose from AIN, AOS, CGM, DSC, DSS, PLT, SEC, or THR clusters; at least two cluster electives required)
- CS Elective (any upper-division CSCI course not used for cluster)
- CS Cluster Elective (second cluster course)
- Open Elective
- Open Elective
- CSCI 472: Historical and Current Perspectives in Computer Science (writing-intensive; required for 4th or 5th year standing; satisfies one of three required writing-intensive courses)
- Gen Ed Elective
- Gen Ed: Immersion (completion of 3-course Immersion sequence)
- Semester Co-op (CSCI 499; additional rotations as scheduled)

---

COURSE COLOR/TYPE LEGEND

- Computer Science / Software Engineering courses: core CS curriculum (CSCI prefix)
- Math courses: MATH sequence supporting CS foundations
- General Education: perspective, immersion, and writing requirements
- Science: Lab Science sequences (Biology, Chemistry, or Physics)
- Open Elective: any credit-bearing course at RIT
- First Year Requirement: YOPS 10 and RIT 365
- Wellness: physical education or wellness activity
- Co-op: CSCI 99 (seminar, every semester), CSCI 488 (summer co-op), CSCI 499 (semester co-op)

---

KEY NOTES

Writing-Intensive Courses: Three are required. The first-year writing course (URWT 150 or ISTE 110) counts as one. One Gen Ed course must be writing-intensive. CSCI 472 (Historical and Current Perspectives in Computer Science) is the program-specific writing-intensive course and must be completed.

Lab Sciences: Only specific sequences are allowed. Biology, Chemistry, and Physics sequences each have approved two-course progressions. See the CS Undergraduate Handbook for the list of allowed sequences.

Perspectives: Lab Science 1 fulfills the required Natural Science Inquiry Perspective. The Calculus sequence (MATH 181 and MATH 182) fulfills the Mathematical Perspectives requirement.

Prerequisites: Prerequisites are shown in parentheses under course names on the official flowchart. Students must satisfy prerequisites before enrolling. The CS department enforces prerequisite requirements and does not routinely grant exceptions.

Co-op Eligibility: Students must maintain satisfactory academic progress and complete co-op seminar (CSCI 99) requirements each semester to remain eligible for co-op rotations. Missing co-op seminar deadlines can delay co-op placement.
