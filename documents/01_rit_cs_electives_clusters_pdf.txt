Computer Science Electives and Clusters
Computer Science electives belong to one or more groupings called clusters. Undergraduate
students with the proper prerequisites are permitted and encouraged to take graduate-level
Computer Science courses. Students should note, however, that in some cases, a graduate-level
course should not be selected if the student has completed a similar undergraduate-level
course. Such details may be found in the course catalog description. The CS Undergraduate
Handbook also contains a document listing pairs of similar graduate and undergraduate
courses.
Some course numbers are generic and represent vehicles for faculty to introduce new courses
in one or perhaps more than one cluster. At the undergraduate level there are seminar
courses and at the graduate level there are topics courses. The entries for these generic
course numbers, for example, CSCI 539 or CSCI 759, are identified as generic in the tables
below and show the potential cluster(s) that might be associated with individual offerings of
these generic course numbers. When specific seminar or topic instances are approved to be
offered, we list them here and indicate the actual cluster or clusters they are associated with.
Be sure to consult the notes table that follows the listings of undergraduate and graduate
Computer Science Electives for important additional information and restrictions.
Vertically Integrated Project (VIP) course offerings provide an opportunity for students to
engage in ambitious, long-term, large-scale multidisciplinary project teams led by faculty. More
information about how participation in a VIP counts toward a Computer Science BS degree is
found in the CS Undergraduate Handbook.

Notes Containing Additional Information or Restrictions
Note Information/Restrictions
1 Undergraduate students who take both CSCI 320 and CSCI 420/420H are not
permitted to take CSCI 620 as a Computer Science elective or for credit toward
a Computer Science degree. Many graduate-level Data Science courses that
require CSCI 620 as a prerequisite also permit students to enroll if they have
completed CSCI 320 and CSCI 420/420H. Some graduate-level Data Science
courses that require CSCI 620 as a prerequisite will permit students to enroll if
they have completed just CSCI 320.
2 CSCI 541 and CSCI 641 are co-listed. Students are permitted to take the courses
for credit more than once, provided each instance deals with a different
paradigm and language.
3 CSCI 589 Undergraduate Thesis Proposal and Preparation, and CSCI 590
Undergraduate Thesis are opportunities for undergraduate students to pursue
an undergraduate thesis. In the first course, the student develops the thesis
proposal; in the second course the student produces the thesis. In each case, a
student must work with a faculty member who agrees to mentor the student.
The proposal form offers the student and faculty member a chance to propose
that the thesis fit in no cluster or be associated with one specific cluster – the
proposed cluster designation is reviewed by the coordinators when the
proposal form is submitted. Approval of the thesis proposal includes approval
of the proposed cluster designation, if one is specified. More information on
CSCI 589 and CSCI 590 is available in the CS Undergraduate Handbook.
4 Independent study projects are proposed by a student working in conjunction
with a faculty member who agrees to sponsor the project. The proposal form
offers the student and faculty member a chance to propose that the project fit
in no cluster or be associated with one specific cluster – the proposed cluster
designation is reviewed by the coordinators when the proposal form is
submitted. Approval of the independent study project includes approval of the
proposed cluster designation, if one is specified. More information on CSCI 599
Computer Science Undergraduate Independent Study is available in the CS
Undergraduate Handbook.
5 CSCI 787 Masters Thesis Proposal and Preparation is for graduate students
who plan to pursue the thesis option, and will apply a course in their program
of study devoted to preparation of the thesis proposal. A student must work
with a faculty member who agrees to mentor the student. The proposal form
offers the student and faculty member a chance to propose that the thesis fit in
no cluster or be associated with one specific cluster – the proposed cluster
designation is reviewed by the coordinators when the proposal form is
submitted. Approval of the thesis proposal includes approval of the proposed
cluster designation, if one is specified. More information on CSCI 787 is
available in the CS Undergraduate Handbook.
